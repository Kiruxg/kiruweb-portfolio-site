PhotoSpace

  View Project   View Code

Project Overview

Photospace is a freelance project that I’ve worked with a client to research, design, and develop a website where photographers can showcase their best work for potential clients. This web application serves to connect professional photographers to clients looking for quality prints. Photospace fosters a collaborative medium for photographers to learn and network with other professionals by sharing their experiences. Over the course of the project, I was responsible for project planning, design, and the programming skills needed to implement a working prototype.

Problem Statement

Photography is an extremely competitive field that requires talent, skills, marketing, and discipline to distinguish oneself as a professional photographer. Freelance creative professionals initially struggle to get the clients and referrals they need to thrive in their small businesses. Photographers early in their profession often lack access to a network of other creatives that provide opportunities for referrals and new ideas to occur.

User Research and Needfinding

The demographic I was targeting were business owners who are professional photographers. I interviewed a freelance photographer and he stressed that he was not attracting enough clients. The photographer is slowly making their mark in their profession and wants to collaborate with other creatives. Below are the most pertinent needs that I have gathered from the interview:

  • Book more clients
  • Network with other creatives to share knowledge, camera gear

By considering the user needs, the goal is to design a platform for professional photographers to interact with potential clients and other freelancing creatives.

Low-Fidelity Prototypes

The second stakeholder to the platform is a user looking for photography services. Once a user arrives on the front page, they can browse for the type of services they are looking for, see profiles of local freelancers near their area. Users also have the option to book small business owners and connect with them in the business profile area. Another top need that freelancers voiced was the desire to share information and equipment with other business owners. By fostering a collaborative blogging medium, creatives are encouraged to form a healthy relationship and potentially acquire more referrals.

Reflection

The primary goal prior to beginning the programming phase was to understand what features were most beneficial to my stakeholders. Freelance photographers are looking to book more clients while having a medium to network with other creatives to share knowledge and camera equipment. The UI had to be simplistic and intuitive, and some features that I felt was needed did not align with user goals. For example, I decided that the ability to like/love a picture is not conducive to the main purpose of PhotoSpace. Users seeking photographer services can get a quick snapshot of each freelancer’s specialties and book them in as little clicks as possible. The website features a photo collection and testimonials for each business owner. Professional photographers looking for connections can interact with other freelancers, through the light blogging medium and comments area which maintains engagement.

The BlogSpace is a medium for the business to provide commentary on photography and another opportunity to connect with clients. The page can allow the photographer to establish meaningful relationships with existing clients and other freelancers looking to obtain knowledge.
